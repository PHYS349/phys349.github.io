# Plans for the Last Few Weeks of PHYS 349

Hi everyone —

Here's how the rest of the semester is going to run. **There is no final exam.** Instead, you'll work on a two-phase final project that replaces it. The project is designed to get you onto real IBM quantum hardware and see first-hand how actual devices deviate from the textbook ideal.

## Overview

The project has two phases:

- **Phase 1 (individual, 50 pts):** Prepare two Bell states on a real IBM quantum processor, measure them in the Z and X bases, and compare to a perfect simulator. This is a short warm-up — a few hours of work — that makes sure everyone has gone through the full IBM Qiskit Runtime loop before team work starts.
- **Phase 2 (team of 2–3, 150 pts):** Pick one of five menu projects, run it on real hardware, and present your results to the class. Projects include studying Grover's performance vs. noise, building small error-correction codes, mapping entanglement quality across a chip, measuring teleportation fidelity, and running Deutsch–Jozsa / Bernstein–Vazirani at scale.

**Total: 200 points** (equivalent to two homework assignments, and this replaces the final exam).

Full project description: [Final Project page on the course website](../lectures/final_project.md).
